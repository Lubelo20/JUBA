3. What the redesign delivers

The redesign keeps all of the above but presents it through a modern, professional, fast and mobile-first experience.

Design direction — "Modern Minimal"

  • Clean, confident layout with generous spacing and clear hierarchy.
  • Brand palette: Navy #0B2E6D, Navy-dark #071d45, Gold #C9A34E.
  • Typography: Poppins — modern, friendly, highly legible.
  • Consistent, reusable component system so every page feels like one product.

Improvements over the current site

  • Modern, cohesive visual identity — a single design system instead of mixed styles.
  • Mobile-first & fully responsive — adapts cleanly from desktop → tablet → phone (3 → 2 → 1 column layouts).
  • New media Gallery — an 8-image grid with an accessible lightbox, plus 8 short videos that load only when played.
  • Performance — no heavy frameworks; images lazy-load; videos use lightweight poster frames.
  • Accessibility — keyboard-navigable, visible focus states, descriptive alt text and ARIA labels.
  • Working enquiry form — submissions handled and validated, ready to wire to Juba's inbox.
  • Maintainable foundation — clean HTML/CSS/JS that any developer can extend.

4. This is a mockup — what that means

The prototype exists to agree the design and structure before we invest in the final build. In the mockup:

  • Photos and videos are placeholders (taken from recent sessions on a phone).
  • Some copy is drafted / illustrative, not final approved wording.
  • Contact details and social links use placeholder values pending confirmation.
  • The AI / ICT / Incubation sections are outlined but not yet built out.

None of this is a limitation of the approach — it is the normal prototype stage. Once you approve direction, we replace placeholders with the real thing.

6. What we need from Juba to continue

To move from mockup to a finished, professional site, please provide the following. This is the single biggest factor in how fast and how well the final site comes together.

A. Company profile & copy

  • Official company profile (PDF/Word) — history, mission, vision, values.
  • Final approved wording for each service and division.
  • Founder / leadership bios and roles.
  • Any accreditations to display (e.g. SETA, QCTO) with reference numbers.

B. Brand assets

  • Logo files in high resolution — ideally vector (SVG/AI/EPS) plus PNG.
  • Brand guidelines if they exist (exact colours, fonts, usage rules).
  • Confirmation of the brand colour palette (currently navy + gold).

C. Photography & video

  • Professional photos of the team, offices, workshops and events (the mockup uses phone snapshots as placeholders).
  • Team headshots (consistent style).
  • Any promotional / training videos to feature.
  • Permission/consent for any images of identifiable people.
